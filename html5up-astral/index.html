CResearch Projects

Structural and functional analysis of cancer-associated missense variants in the retinoblastoma protein (Rb) pocket domain

This study investigates the cryptic effects of 75 cancer-associated missense mutations on the pocket domain of the retinoblastoma tumor suppressor protein (Rb), essential for cell cycle regulation. We found that many of these mutations destabilize the protein's structure and reduce its binding affinity with E2F transcription factors and LxCxE motif-containing repressors, even when the mutations are not at the direct binding sites. Through functional assays and X-ray crystallography, the work reveals multiple ways these mutations alter Rb's stability and function, offering new insights into how such alterations contribute to cancer development.

Regulatory ligand binding in plant chalcone isomeraseâ€“like (CHIL) proteins

Chalcone isomerase-like (CHIL) proteins enhance flavonoid production in plants by interacting with chalcone synthase (CHS) through protein-protein interactions, favoring naringenin chalcone production. Using Vitis vinifera CHIL (VvCHIL), we found that metabolites like naringenin chalcone and naringenin influence CHIL thermostability and its binding with CHS, suggesting CHIL acts as a metabolite sensor for pathway feedback. Structural analysis identified amino acid differences that modulate these effects, highlighting CHIL's role in regulating flavonoid biosynthesis.
